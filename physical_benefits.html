What can you gain PHYSICALLY from sports and exercise

Helps manage weight

Not only does exercise help burn calories directly, Muscle cells need a lot of energy, which means they burn a lot of calories. In fact, they burn more calories than fat cells, even when you're not exercising. So the time you spend working out reaps benefits long after you stop sweating. So the time you spend working out reaps benefits long after you stop sweating.

Improves sleep

Exercise can increase time spent in deep sleep, increase time spent sleeping, and even help with insomnia and sleep disorders

Reduced blood pressure

Physical activity keeps your heart and blood vessels healthy, helping to prevent hypertension.

Lowers risk of heart disease

Exercise also increases your levels of HDL cholesterol that lowers heart disease risk by flushing the artery-clogging LDL out of your system

Lower risk of diabetes

Being active makes your body more sensitive to insulin (the hormone that allows cells in your body to use blood sugar for energy), which helps manage your diabetes

Improved cardiovascular health

The heart is a muscle, it needs to be worked out just like any other muscle and regular exercise can help improve the overall health of your entire cardiovascular system

Ward off osteoporosis

Exercise helps you to Build dense and strong bones which can prevent osteoporosis
